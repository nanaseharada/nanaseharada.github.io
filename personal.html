What I belive

    Last, but not least - I became a protestant Christian when I was in a graduate school in the U.S. In Japan, some people probably see religions synonymous with cults. While a true religion simply tries to figure out what the world view should be, cults are created to benefit certain individuals. There is a clear difference between the two, but the concerns are understandable given that it is often hard to discern them. Even "decent" religions can be dangerous. Religions unavoidably provide moral standards. Some religious people make mistakes by applying the black and white standard to grey zone issues. That said, I am attracted to the character of Jesus Christ, and how he served and loved. In fact, true Christianity is supposed to free people from blindly applying religious rules and bring life to what the rules are made for - to love. Here is what I believe.
  • There is a Creater God. Even an atheist Friedrich Nietzshe said that you cannot argue an absolute moral standard unless you assume there is God. If the life of a loved one is brutally taken, you would want to argue that it is "wrong", not just that it is unacceptable in the modern society. (You may have a different oponion, of course.)
