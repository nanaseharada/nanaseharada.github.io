What I belive

    Last, but not least - I became a protestant Christian when I was in a graduate school in the U.S. In Japan, some people probably see religions synonymous with cults. While a true religion simply tries to figure out what the world view should be, cults are created to benefit certain individuals. There is a clear difference between the two, but I understand if someone is concerned given that it is often hard to discern them. Even "decent" religions can be dangerous. Religions unavoidably provide moral standards. Some religious people make mistakes by applying the black-and-white standard to grey zone issues, which can be hurtful. That said, I am attracted to the character of Jesus Christ, and how he served and loved. In fact, true Christianity is supposed to free people from blindly applying religious rules and bring life to what the rules are made for - to love. Here is some of what I believe and what I hope to reflect in my professional life.
  • There is God, who created life and the physical world. Physics can explain how the Universe evolved to some extent. Chemistry and physics can explain some of the materials that exist in space and on earth. Even prebiotic molecules like amino acids have been discovered in space (on comets). However, physics and chemistry do a TERRIBLE job explaining life. Right before and after we take our last breath, there should be no significant change in our bodies in terms of chemistry. Yet, we know that there is a vast difference spiritually. What brought life into existence? A Christian belief is that God created this Universe and life (Genesis 1). While this may sound like assertion unsupported by evidence, I don't think it is too crazy to consider that there is a source of life outside of our current understanding of science.
  • Every single person is precious.
    • Up to a certain age, this is the narrative we hear. We should respect everyone. As we grow up, the society starts to tell you that you have no worth unless you are sucessful. The Bible tells you that we are created in the image of God (Genesis 1:27), we are "fearfully and wonderfully made," (Psalms 139:14), and we are God's materpiece (Ephesians 2:10). It is meaningful to work hard and succeed, but it is not the goal for Christians - it is more about what to do with your success.
  • Leaders should be servants.
    • Leaders usually have more responsibilities and burdens, so they do deserve some respect. I am not saying that we don't need to support or appreciate them. Meanwhile, leaders do have power, and some leaders abuse them. The Bible says "The first shall be the last and the last shall be the first" (Matthew 20:16). If you try to elevate yourself, you will be brought down. If you humble yourself, you will be elevated. The Christ, who is meant to be the Son of God and the king, came to serve.
  • There is an absolute moral standard. Even an atheist Friedrich Nietzshe said that you cannot argue an absolute moral standard unless you assume there is God. Many people may actually be OK saying that moral standards are subjective, and people can have different standards. But if the life of a loved one is brutally taken, then you would likely want to argue that it is "wrong", not just that it is unacceptable in the modern society.
